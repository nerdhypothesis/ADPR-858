5 Reasons Why Hootsuite Blog Works for My Social Media Queries

If you are a social media communicator, there are a few platforms that should be your go-to destination for seeking solutions and keeping yourself up-to-date. The Hootsuite blog is one such platform that I often refer to. Here are five reasons why I visit the blog frequently.

1. The keywords you look for

To start with Hootsuite blog uses headlines that are likely to include keywords that we use for searching information related to our social media operations — “What is User-Generated Content? And Why is it Important?” So when you type your social media-related query, Hootsuite is right there with matching search results.

2. Breaking down the solutions

Hootsuite breaks down the problem statement into multiple sections, and each section contains independent solutions. As you can see in the blog post, the problem statement of ‘How to manage multiple social media accounts‘ has been segregated into ten distinct sections.

And guess what, each section is hyperlinked right at the introductory paragraph. So if you do not want to spend time reading the entire blog, you may just read the sections of your choice.

3. Smart presentation of large information

In the blog the problems and the context are discussed using brief text and infographics. But wait, if you are interested to know more, you can refer to cross-links. The solution is presented in both text and video formats for easy understanding.

4. Measurable indicators-based solutions

The blog explains how you can use Hootsuite as a one-stop solution for social media management. It also includes an overview of the work-flow process along with measurable indicators that would give you a clear idea about the performance output you would desire.

5. Offering solutions to non-subscribers

While Hootsuite blog posts market the platform, the blog offers a significant volume of literature that could help address your social media bottlenecks. You do not even need to subscribe to it to access the vast range of solutions and information

That is how the blog acquires a captive readership who are aware of the platform and even help advocate it. I just did that, didn’t I?
