Crime

The Missing Native American Boys of Nebraska

For every Native American girl missing in Nebraska, two Native boys are missing.

More than two-thirds of missing Native Americans in Nebraska are boys under the age of 17 years as per a study by researchers at the University of Nebraska Omaha.

The findings from the study further showed missing Native American boys in Nebraska are outpacing the missing person rate for White people and other races and are second only to Black people.

Nebraska Foster Care Review office observed a high prevalence of mental health challenges and substance use among the children that were further aggravated due to domestic violence, poverty and lack of affordable housing/homelessness.

Economic Development

Winnebago Homeownership Changing Lives

Like many Native American communities, housing is a critical priority for Nebraska's Winnebago Indian Tribe. Their economic development corporation Ho-Chunk Inc. is answering the call with custom-built homes and other community facilities along with downpayment assistance and homebuyer education, creating an environment that was unimaginable even ten years back.
